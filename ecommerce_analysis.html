Conclusion:

  • The company's performance is growing over time but dropps considerably in December. This seems a seasonal effect but the performance in December of previous year is a bit better;
  • In November, the company had a peak in sales with more than 1.4M in revenue;
  • United Kingdom (UK) is the main market. It contributes to 85% of the company revenue;
  • UK has the min revenue per month around 400k. Netherlands and EIRE (Ireland), 2nd and 3th in revenue, have the max revenue per month around 40k.
  • AOV had a peak in December for UK. The reason is because the orders dropped around 70% while the revenue dropped 55% in that month;
  • The top 6 sold products overall is the same top 6 sold products for UK. The contribution of them together is around 5% of the UK revenue;
  • The top 2 products were sold only in a certain month, not continuously. The other 8 remaining products (from the top 10) keep the performance more consistent;
  • In November, the company had a peak in sales because, based on the top 10 sold products for UK, people were more interested in Christmas shopping. Also, the top 1 product is one of the top 10 sold products for UK and overall;
  • Returned users contribute more to the UK revenue than new users for the entire period;
  • As the number of total users dropped around 63% and the revenue dropped around 55% in December for UK, the average revenue per customer increased in that month.
